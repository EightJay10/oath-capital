01The Process

Four steps.
Full transparency.

Every investor who works with Oath Capital goes through the same deliberate process. We do not rush capital deployment. We do not skip steps. The process exists to protect your investment — and ours.

1
Step One
Investor Inquiry & Strategy Call

The process begins with a brief strategy call with our investor relations team. This is not a sales call — it is a qualification conversation. We want to understand your goals, timeline, and risk tolerance. You will leave knowing exactly whether Oath Capital is the right fit for your portfolio.

20–30 minute call, no obligation
We review your accreditation status and investment objectives
You receive our full investor documentation package
If it is not a fit, we will tell you directly
2
Step Two
Review the Deal & Offering Documents

When a deal becomes available, qualified investors receive the full offering memorandum — not a teaser deck. This includes the business plan, financial projections (base, conservative, and stress-case), market analysis, operator background, and full fee disclosure.

Complete offering memorandum with all financial details
Three-scenario underwriting: base, conservative, and stress
Full fee and promote structure disclosed upfront
Q&A session available with our team before committing
3
Step Three
Commit Capital & Complete Subscription

If you decide to invest, we guide you through the subscription process — entity setup if needed, wire instructions, and legal documentation. Our team is available throughout. Most investors complete this process in under a week.

Subscription agreement and investor questionnaire
Invest via personal account, trust, LLC, or self-directed IRA
Secure wire transfer with written confirmation
Dedicated support from our team at every step
4
Step Four
Receive Distributions & Quarterly Reports

Once the asset is acquired and stabilized, investors receive regular cash distributions per the waterfall structure outlined in the offering documents. Every quarter you receive a full performance report — actual figures, not summaries — showing how the asset tracks against projections.

Quarterly cash distributions (deal-dependent)
Detailed quarterly reports: actuals vs. projections
Annual K-1 tax documentation
Direct line to our team throughout the hold period

03Why Commercial Real Estate

The case for multi-family
as an asset class.

01
Inflation-resistant income

Residential leases renew annually, allowing rents to adjust with inflation. Unlike fixed-income instruments, multi-family assets can grow revenue in real terms — performing well in inflationary environments where bonds and cash erode.

02
Durable, needs-based demand

Housing is not discretionary. People need a place to live regardless of economic cycles. Multi-family assets benefit from structural demand that does not exist for retail, office, or hospitality — asset classes far more exposed to behavioral and cyclical risk.

03
Significant tax advantages

Through cost segregation and bonus depreciation, investors can generate substantial paper losses that offset passive income. Depreciation benefits are one of the primary reasons accredited investors hold real estate alongside equities and bonds.

04
Operator-driven value creation

Unlike publicly traded securities, private multi-family investments allow skilled operators to actively create value through renovations, operational improvements, and lease-up execution. Returns are not solely dependent on market movements.

05
Low correlation to public markets

Private real estate does not trade daily. For investors with meaningful stock and bond exposure, multi-family real estate adds genuine diversification — real assets with real cash flow, not just a different ticker on the same market.

06
Long-term wealth compounding

Between recurring cash flow, principal paydown through debt amortization, appreciation over a 5–7 year hold, and tax-deferred refinancing strategies, multi-family real estate compounds investor wealth through multiple simultaneous mechanisms.
