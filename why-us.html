02Our Differentiators

Four things we do
differently.

I
Track Record & Returns
We show you the numbers. All of them.

We present three underwriting scenarios — base, conservative, and stress — for every deal. Our investors see actual performance against projections in every quarterly report. We do not cherry-pick strong results. We document what happened and explain why.

All historical returns disclosed to prospective investors
Quarterly actuals vs. projections, no summaries
Conservative underwriting targets met in downside scenarios
II
Transparent Reporting
You always know exactly where you stand.

Most operators send brief quarterly emails with occupancy rates and a reassuring tone. Oath Capital sends detailed reports: actual NOI vs. underwritten NOI, expense variances, capital expenditure tracking, and a candid assessment of where the asset stands relative to the business plan.

Full financial statements, not summaries
Variance analysis with written explanations
Annual K-1s delivered on time, every year
III
Experienced Team
Small team. Deep expertise. No juniors on your deal.

Oath Capital is deliberately small. We do not scale by hiring junior analysts to manage investor relations or underwrite deals. Every deal is evaluated by our principals. Every investor interaction is handled by a decision-maker. You know exactly who is managing your capital.

All deals underwritten and managed by principals
Direct access to the team throughout the hold
Strategic guidance from senior advisors with deep market expertise
IV
Conservative Underwriting
We model for what could go wrong — not just what should go right.

Our target returns are achievable in the conservative scenario, not just the optimistic one. We stress-test every deal for occupancy declines, expense overruns, and exit cap rate expansion. We apply a 20% adverse condition test before committing any capital. If it does not pass, we do not do the deal.

20% adverse condition test on every acquisition
Exit cap rate conservatively underwritten above entry cap rate
Debt structure reviewed for rate risk and refinance risk
