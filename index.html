01 Who We Are

An operator
built on
accountability.

Oath Capital is a private real estate investment firm focused exclusively on multi-family acquisitions in high-growth U.S. markets. We acquire, reposition, and operate apartment communities on behalf of accredited investors who want institutional-quality returns without institutional-sized minimums.

We chose the name deliberately. An oath is a commitment — to performance, to transparency, and to treating our investors' capital with the same discipline we would apply to our own. Every member of our investment committee holds a personal LP position in every deal we sponsor.

02 How It Works

From first
conversation to
first distribution.

Investing with Oath is a deliberate process — for both sides. We take time to understand your goals. You take time to understand ours. When the fit is right, we move with precision. Here is what that looks like.

01

Join the Investor List

Submit your information to join our private investor network. You will receive our research, deal pipeline updates, and early access to offerings before they open broadly.

02

Strategy Call

A 20-minute conversation with our investor relations team. We review your goals, investment timeline, and capital capacity. If this is not the right fit, we will say so clearly.

03

Review the Deal

Qualified investors receive the full offering memorandum: market thesis, underwriting assumptions, projected returns, fee structure, and management team bios. No black boxes.

04

Invest & Receive

Commit capital, execute the subscription agreement, and receive quarterly distributions and reports throughout the hold. Typically a 3–7 year value-add strategy with a defined exit.

Our current minimum is $100,000 per deal. This may vary depending on the specific offering and available allocation. We maintain a smaller number of investors per deal deliberately — it allows us to communicate more meaningfully and allocate more thoughtfully.
Yes. All Oath Capital offerings are available exclusively to accredited investors as defined by the SEC — individuals with a net worth exceeding $1M (excluding primary residence) or annual income exceeding $200K ($300K jointly) for the past two consecutive years. We verify accreditation as part of our onboarding process.
Our deals are structured as value-add strategies with a typical hold period of 3 to 7 years. We underwrite to a defined exit — either a sale or refinance — and communicate projected timelines clearly in the offering memorandum. We do not extend holds without investor communication and a clear rationale.
We do not guarantee returns and past performance does not predict future results. Historically, our deals have targeted net IRRs in the range of 15–20% with preferred returns to LPs. Every offering memorandum includes detailed underwriting with base, conservative, and stress-case projections. We encourage you to scrutinize them.
Distributions are made quarterly, typically beginning once the property reaches stabilized occupancy following acquisition and initial renovations. The distribution schedule, preferred return structure, and waterfall are outlined in full in each offering memorandum before you commit a dollar.
