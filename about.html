01 Investment Philosophy

We invest where
the math
demands it.

Our investment thesis is not complicated. Population moves toward opportunity. Opportunity requires housing. Multi-family real estate, acquired and operated well, captures that demand — and holds value through cycles that punish more speculative strategies.

We do not chase trends. We do not buy in markets simply because they are popular. We follow a disciplined six-factor market filter, underwrite conservatively, and accept that the right answer is often to wait.

Every decision we make is governed by a single question: would we still be comfortable with this if conditions deteriorated by 20%? If the answer is no, we pass.

"The goal is not to find the best deal available. It is to find the deals worth doing — and have the discipline to ignore everything else."
I
Market before property
A mediocre asset in a high-growth market will outperform an excellent one in the wrong city. We spend the majority of our underwriting time on the macro thesis before we evaluate a single address.
II
Conservative projections, always
We underwrite to base, conservative, and stress-case scenarios. We present all three to investors. Our target returns are achievable in the conservative case — not only in the optimistic one.
III
Aligned capital structure
Our promote is back-loaded. We earn our upside when investors earn theirs — not before. Every principal at Oath holds a personal LP position in every deal we sponsor.
IV
Defined exit, from day one
We enter every deal knowing how we intend to exit and on what timeline. Hold period, exit trigger, and disposition strategy are documented in the offering memorandum before a dollar is committed.

Why Oath Capital
The name was chosen deliberately
Most firms in private real estate name themselves after geography, heritage, or aspirational concepts. We chose a word that means exactly what it says. An oath is a binding commitment — not a best-effort promise.
What we are bound to
To our investors: accurate underwriting, transparent reporting, and honest communication when deals do not go to plan. To our residents: well-maintained housing and competent management. To our team: a firm that holds itself to the same standard it asks of others.
Why it matters in this industry
Private real estate is an industry with low transparency and limited accountability. We believe that operators who behave differently — who communicate with discipline and structure incentives honestly — build better long-term businesses. That is the bet we are making.

03 Culture & Values

How we behave
when no one
is watching.

◈
Discipline over opportunity

We pass on more deals than we pursue. The ability to say no — clearly and without apology — is the most important skill in this business. We have built a culture that rewards restraint, not activity.

◉
Transparency by default

When a deal underperforms, we say so first and explain why. Our quarterly reports show actual figures against projections — not summaries, not spin. We believe investors who receive honest information make better decisions.

◎
Long-term thinking

Our reputation is the only real asset we own. Every decision is made with that in mind. We will not cut corners on due diligence, investor communication, or property management to accelerate short-term returns.

◇
Ownership at every level

We do not manage other people's risk from the sidelines. Every principal holds personal capital in every deal. When we say our interests are aligned with our investors', we mean it in the most literal sense.

◈
Precise communication

We write clearly. We respond promptly. We do not use jargon to obscure uncertainty. If we do not know the answer to a question, we say so — then find it. Our investors are treated as intelligent adults.

◉
Candor over comfort

We will tell an investor if we think a deal is not right for them. We will tell a seller if their price does not work. We will tell ourselves the truth about a thesis before we commit capital to it. Comfort is not a value we optimize for.
